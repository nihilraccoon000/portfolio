The Journey of A Bad Design Lover

Well, what is this all about?

This is basically just a listing of the assignments I've done for a UI/UX class.

My confession:

I think of myself as a bad design lover who would constantly check back at a stream where this genious VTuber introduced himself with a terrible PowerPoint I laugh so hard everytime I watch it.

For the sake of maintaining the unthoughtful joy, it is probably not a good idea to try to analyze why I like these bad designs. But I guess what makes them interesting (somehow funny, but also nostalgic and cute) to me is how they dump nowadays aesthetics and embrace the outdated ones. I am forgetful, I tend to take the modern, industrial styles around me for granted. Without looking at these "bad designs", it is hard to notice how much design as whole (including aesthetics, usability, etc.) has evolved throughout the past 10 to 20 years.

To produce good (up-to-date) designs is not that easy. I am, after all, not yet a good designer and definitely not a talented one. In this portfolio, you'll find
  • some practice on some common practices in UI/UX design
  • inmature efforts to improve the usability of an outdated website
  • more inmature efforts to design a prototype
  • a website for my favorite character, plus the invisible struggles to make it fairly usable and not too ugly (but also not too "modern", though it should hardly be my concern)

Last words: I know this introduction somehow represents a bad design itself, so thanks for bearing with me. If you're still interested after reading this wordy intro, without further ado, scroll down and take a look at the projects :)
